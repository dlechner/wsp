Common Nintendo Wii Errors & Malfunctions

  • Red Light: This is a severe hardware failure. Complete mainboard and system diagnostics are required to determine the root cause
  • Flashing Blue Light: This is your Wii talking to the Nintendo network through the internet. Usually means its updating the system or you have messages.
  • Loud Noise: A loud spinning/grinding noise can be heard. The disk drive is faulty and will need to be replaced soon as it can start damaging game discs.
  • No Lights: The power supply cable is loose or malfunctioning. Verify proper connection, if still no power/lights then try a new power cable. If the problem still persists then a diagnostics will be needed.
  • Disc Drive-Will not read, will not accept, or will not eject discs: Disc may be dirty, try cleaning the disc or trying a different disc. If the problem persists then it may be a faulty disc drive and would need to be replaced.
  • 32030: Nintendo WI-FI servers are down for maintenance. Try again at a later time.
  • 32003: Your console memory is full. Try deleting some files or transferring some to a SD Card.
  • 51330: Wireless Router Password Error: The password/key for your wireless router was entered incorrectly, verify password. If problem persists then you would need to troubleshoot your router and settings.

If problems persist with any of the above codes a full diagnostics is needed to confirm and repair accordingly.
